Fundamental Concepts

Encapsulation

Encapsulation describes the idea of wrapping data and all the methods that work on said data, within one unit.

Inheritance

Inheritance is the capability of one class to derive or inherit properties from another class

Polymorphism

Polymorphism the word means having many forms. In programming, polymorphism means having the same function name (but different signatures) being used for different types.

Abstraction

Abstract classes can be considered as blueprints for other classes. It allows a set of methods to be created within child classes that are built from the abstract class. A class with one or more abstract methods is an abstract class.

Excite similarly had an automated keyword search engine. These two companies were in a battle for search space. They started to add non-search features to compete, and search engine quality suffered.

Google It

It's All About the Links.

Google was founded by Larry Page and Sergey Brin in 1998. They combined keyword search with the number of links that a website has from other sites, in other words gauging its Internet "popularity" and usefulness.

Overture.com

Who Needs Banner Ads

Overture.com was the first to introduce targeted ad results and to sell ad space to companies in order to get their results to be higher on the list of certain keyword searches.

Google Refuses Banner Ads

Google insists on keeping it's page simple, and banner free. It's search engine based on link usefulness is innovative, but they need a way to generate revenue. Venture capitalists investing in Google apply pressure to find a revenue source.

Can A Deal Be Made

Google and Overture.com are brought together to make a deal. It's not truly known what happens, but nothing comes of it. Although, Google does go ahead with it's own targeted ad results engine which effectively copies Overture.com. Overture then sues Google and wins an unknown settlement. Overture.com founder Bill Gross obtains an undisclosed amount of Google stock.

Google IPO To The Moon

Google launches it's IPO in 2004, after the dot com bubble and still does well. The stock remained a high growth stock up to even today. Their combination of keyword, link popularity, and targeted ad results, have allowed them to expand into the juggernaut of today.
